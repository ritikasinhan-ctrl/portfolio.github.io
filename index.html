GROUP PROJECT · SEMESTER 2 · ICDT1016Y — COMMUNICATION & BUSINESS SKILLS
Clinic Appointment Booking System — Group Synopsis

A group academic writing project in which we produced a comprehensive synopsis for a web-based Clinic Appointment Booking System using the Django framework. The system was designed to address the inefficiencies of manual appointment management in Mauritian clinics — including scheduling conflicts, high administrative workload, and limited patient access outside office hours.

My specific contributions covered the Statement of the Problem — detailing issues such as manual workload, double bookings, lack of 24/7 access, and no-shows — as well as the Technology Potentials section, which analysed web frameworks, database technologies, notification services, and cloud hosting options. I also compiled all Harvard references for the group submission.

Academic Writing Harvard Referencing Problem Analysis Technical Research Group Collaboration

GROUP ASSIGNMENT · SEMESTER 2 · ICDT1202Y — DATABASE SYSTEMS
Hotel Management System — Database Design

A database systems group assignment in which we designed and implemented a full Hotel Management System database. The project involved complete normalisation from UNF through to 3NF, an Entity Relationship Diagram, 17 fully specified database tables covering guests, rooms, reservations, restaurant orders, billing, and staff management, 15 stored procedures ranging from basic to expert complexity, 7 triggers enforcing business rules such as double-booking prevention and automatic room status updates, role-based access control using DCL, and a comprehensive indexing strategy.

I contributed equally (20%) across the design and implementation. This was the most technically demanding project of the year and gave me deep, practical experience in database architecture and SQL programming.

SQL / T-SQL Database Normalisation ERD Design Stored Procedures Triggers DCL & Security Indexing

REFLECTION

My Learning Journey

An honest reflection on the challenges I faced, the skills I developed, and the lessons I carry forward.

"This module has taught me that technical skill alone is not enough — the ability to communicate clearly, collaborate openly, and reflect honestly is just as important in building a meaningful career."

Challenges I faced

One of the most consistent challenges this year was stepping outside my comfort zone in collaborative and communicative settings. As a naturally introverted person, contributing actively in group work and delivering presentations required deliberate and conscious effort. The Interview and CRM Role Play was particularly difficult — it demanded that I project confidence in a live, face-to-face context, which I found genuinely uncomfortable at first.

Balancing ICDT1016Y alongside technically demanding modules like Database Systems was also challenging. Managing deadlines across creative group activities, formal academic writing, and complex SQL implementation taught me the importance of planning ahead and maintaining quality across very different types of work at the same time.

Skills I developed

This module has meaningfully developed my professional communication skills. Through the Business Plan, Clinic Booking System synopsis, and Research Paper, I built the ability to write formally, structure arguments clearly, and reference sources correctly using the Harvard system. The group activities — particularly the Scavenger Hunt — helped me become more comfortable contributing in team settings, which is a real area of personal growth.

My experience at MUA before university gave me an early foundation in professional communication. ICDT1016Y helped me articulate and build on that foundation more consciously — connecting what I already knew from the workplace with the communication frameworks studied in the module.

Where I still need to improve

Spoken communication is the area I most want to develop. While I am confident in written communication, verbal communication — particularly in group discussions and presentations to unfamiliar audiences — remains challenging. I intend to address this through continued practice and by seeking opportunities to present and speak in professional settings.

I also recognise that professional networking is an area I need to develop intentionally. As I move toward postgraduate study and a career in cybersecurity or database security, being able to communicate my technical skills clearly to non-technical stakeholders will be essential.

How I will apply this in my career

The skills developed in ICDT1016Y are not supplementary to my technical studies — they are foundational to them. In any technical role, whether in database administration, cybersecurity, or software engineering, the ability to write clearly, communicate professionally, and collaborate effectively is not optional. It is what separates a capable technician from a capable professional.

My time at MUA showed me what that looks like in practice. This module has given me the academic language and reflective awareness to keep developing those skills with intention — and to carry them into every role I take on from here.
